My Favourite Ukrainian Song: Гей, соколи!

About "Гей, соколи!"

"Гей, соколи!" is traditionally considered a Ukrainian folk song, believed to be written by Tomasz Padura, though this attribution remains debated among scholars.1 The song is about a young Cossack saying farewell to his beloved and Ukraine, symbolizing themes of love, separation, and longing for the homeland. It has become an enduring cultural symbol, especially during times of conflict, like the recent Russian invasion of Ukraine, where it was used to boost morale and express solidarity.

According to historical research, the song reflects the broader struggles of the Ukrainian Cossacks during the 17th and 18th centuries, when they frequently fought against foreign powers, such as the Polish-Lithuanian Commonwealth and later the Russian Empire. The Cossack's departure in the song represents their duty to defend their land, often leading to painful separations from their families and loved ones.2
